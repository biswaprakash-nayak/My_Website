ENG2003 Work 1

Video Interview (Assignment 2)

  • Why selected: A communication format without real-time feedback from the audience.
  • Growth shown: Better camera delivery and clearer structured responses.
  • Comfort zone: Yes, this pushed me because I am camera-shy.
  • Best work: A strong baseline that improves with repeated practice.

It is a mock virtual interview where you are given a question and you record your answer on the platform and submit it. This is all done without any external human input and only requires the interviewee to record themselves when they are free before the deadline. Each question usually has a minute or 2 thinking time and then a few minutes to actually record your video.

This is different from most other assignments and traditional forms of communication. Usually we are talking to a person or text or emailing them mainly on a one on one basis. You know who you are talking with. This is very different as you can’t see who is actually looking at your video. This is very disjointed and hence a unique experience in the new digital world. So due to this I chose to include this piece for my showcase.

Majority of my experience has been talking to people directly but never in this way. This is really weird as you have no human response to go off of or anything. This may be similar to making a YouTube video or presenting on TV which may be useful in the future when making project submission videos as well. I am really camera shy and find it difficult to talk to the camera like that and this definitely gave me a new experience.

I am not used to talking to a camera and am very camera shy. This was definitely way out of my comfort zone.

I don’t have much experience with this and considering I have camera shyness, it was the best I could do at that moment of time. I am not sure if I am any better but it would help to get more practice in this.

In the time I had I can say it is the best. I spent the last day or 2 before it was due actually doing the assignment. If I started earlier I could have made it a lot better but then I couldn’t have spent time on my other classes.

ENG2003 Work 3

Group Assignment 5: Presentation

Scroll on the slide area to snap one full slide at a time.

  • Why selected: The major collaborative communication deliverable in the course.
  • Growth shown: Team coordination, shared messaging, and clear section handoff.
  • Comfort zone: Video recording and controlled pacing required repeated retakes.
  • Best work: Strong slide quality with improved delivery over iterations.

We had to make a presentation based on our technical report from part B.

This was the first and only major group work we had and not only a quick tutorial activity. This makes it an actual communication assignment.

It was a breeze working with my team, which means my communication is at a point where I can efficiently work with a random team of people I don’t know.

Communicating with the team was not hard. Again we had to record ourselves speaking to the camera and submit that video. We chose to do it on our own time and put together the video. This was out of my comfort zone as I am not used to talking to a camera without any human like in the interview assignment.

The slides were really good and the presentation was also decent. I had to extend my section so I had to speak slower than usual which made it really difficult for me as it was a lot slower than I am used to, making me think more and losing track often, so I needed to do multiple retakes. If I had more time to practice and do more retakes then it would have been a lot better, but for the time I had it was really good.

Open Project

Cosmik AI Visualizer

Live orbital preview inspired by the Cosmikai visualizer.

  • What it is: AI exoplanet detection project for citizen science.
  • Why selected: Largest ongoing project from hackathon to multi-month build.
  • Growth shown: Professor outreach, capstone collaboration, and team leadership.
  • Comfort zone: The biggest stretch in professional communication so far.
  • Best work: MVP complete, with model training improvements underway.

Cosmik AI is an AI exoplanet detector being developed for citizen science applications to quickly find exoplanets in stars.

This is the largest project I have done to date. It started as a hackathon project and turned into a many month project. We worked with a professor and were a team of students who came up with this. This included finding and talking to professors who would work with us and also making time for it alongside our busy classes.

I was usually scared to approach new people, especially professors. This forced me to talk to a few and find one who is interested in our work. We also partnered with a capstone team and had to talk with them. I had to make sure my teammates understood the technical requirements of their tasks and were on the same page.

Yes this pushed me out of my comfort zone the most. I had to find and talk with a few professors which I was not used to doing and was also afraid of doing. I also got used to talking to professors normally as I had to email the professor often with questions, updates and so on. I also had to work with the capstone team which were people I didn’t know but got to know them better. I also managed my team, telling them their tasks and making sure they delivered on time.

This is not fully complete as the AI model requires more training. This is a long term project but I have a current MVP up on my GitHub. This is for the time being my best work. I am not sure how I can improve it further other than updating the AI model. This takes time and is also not my field as I am more of the backend integrator than the AI developer.

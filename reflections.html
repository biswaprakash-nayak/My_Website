Reflections

Structured as two parts (ENG2003 experience + personal growth) with examples and links to course concepts.

Part 1

ENG2003 Experience

ENG2003 was different from other courses I have done as it was less technical and more on communication and relied on your English skills.

I would say some activities were fun, especially the Kahoots while others felt like a chore and a waste of time. Most tutorials were not bad, the TA was really nice and talking to people and working on the activity was decently fun for most of the time. I found the term project 1, the technical report, to be a good project where we got the chance to research anything and make a report about it. It was fun researching about a topic you are really passionate about and making a report to help others see your passion and the information you are trying to convey. The group assignment where we had to present one of our reports was also quick and enjoyable. Working with a team of random people is not so difficult most of the time. One of the members was being a bit difficult but we resolved it and made, what I would say is a really good looking presentation. On the other side there were activities like the cover letter or some of the tutorial activities which seemed like a drag compared to the other activities and would say they could have been done better. The lecture activities are not so bad, I found a group near the beginning and stuck with them throughout the semester. So, working with my group for the inclass activities was really easy and enjoyable. The best part of the course was definitely the Kahoots, I learnt a lot from them as it was easy to retain important information through activities. Compared to lectures where it gets boring really fast and you start to zone out and miss all the information, these kahoot sessions were definitely a breath of fresh air. This course was a bit different from what I was expecting as I expected a dull lecture and many boring, and not so useful essay writings. This course had some aspects of what I had dreaded but overall it was still better than I expected and is really useful for people who cannot communicate, though the amount of such people may be really low. I would say the technical report, term project 1, was the most useful as I had never written a technical report before. THis gave me the experience of writing one and I learned a lot about writing a report and how to properly cite sources using IEEE from this. Otherwise, the other assignments were more or the same to what I have experienced before and would not say they truly changed something, for me at least. Overall I would say the experience was okay but did not stand out in any way. After completing the course I am not sure how much I would have really missed out on if I did not do the course.

Part 2

Personal Growth

I would say even before ENG2003, I had a strong basis for communication already.

After the course, I don’t think I have really changed much in any sense. Most of the skills like working with others and writing essays or paragraphs are mostly what I have been doing most of my life. If I were to miss out on this course then I would not really lose much, if anything. The 7Cs and the axioms of communications seemed like a beneficial thing but the more I thought about it, the more I came to the conclusion of why do we need it? Most of the points discussed were already common knowledge. Also when you go to communicate, you don’t think about the 7Cs. You go with what seems the best and usually it is good enough for the task. Making communication about strict rules removes nuance and abilities people would have learnt from just communicating. This is useful for someone new to english or someone who is introverted with little communication experience. Coming from my background with ample experience I would say nothing truly improved under this course. The only skill I would say I am lacking is talking to a camera like the virtual interview. Though I am not sure how useful this skill is as most often you will be talking to a human and this seems close to dystopian more than anything. I also have a habit of rambling on and being less concise but I don’t think every piece of writing needs to be super concise. If all writing was concise then there would be no humanity in them, might as well use AI to talk to each other. On top of that, I can be concise accordingly like in my technical report or quick communications between people. I already have a strong basis for technical communications coming from many hackathons or working with professors. I also have pitching and public speaking experience to draw from when it comes to tailoring the information for the crowd. So, in that sense I wouldn’t say anything changed when it comes to approaching other courses differently. Most likely I will forget the 7Cs or the axioms but still remember the overall idea of how to communicate from the experience I have had.
